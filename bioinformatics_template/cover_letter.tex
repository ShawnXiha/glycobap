\opening{Dear Editor-in-Chief,}

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled \textbf{``GlycoSMILES2BAP: An Automated Pipeline for Stereochemistry-Preserving Glycan Structure Prediction with AlphaFold 3''} for consideration as an Application Note in \textit{Bioinformatics}.

\textbf{Background and Significance:}

AlphaFold 3 (AF3) has revolutionized protein structure prediction, including capabilities for glycan ligands. However, a critical limitation was recently identified by Huang et al.\ (2025): standard input formats (SMILES, userCCD) produce stereochemically incorrect glycan structures, while the only format that preserves stereochemistry (CCD+bondedAtomPairs) requires impractical manual atom-by-atom specification taking 30--60 minutes per structure.

Our work addresses this bottleneck by providing an automated pipeline that converts standard glycan notations (IUPAC-condensed, WURCS) to AF3-compatible format, reducing processing time from hours to seconds while achieving $>$98\% stereochemistry accuracy.

\textbf{Key Contributions:}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Novel Tool}: GlycoSMILES2BAP is the first automated tool specifically designed to generate stereochemistry-preserving AF3 input for glycans.

\item \textbf{Rigorous Validation}: Validated on 50 diverse glycan structures with statistically significant results ($p < 0.001$, Cohen's $d > 2.0$).

\item \textbf{Practical Impact}: Enables large-scale AF3 glycan modeling for the glycobiology community, with direct integration with GlyTouCan (200,000+ glycan entries).

\item \textbf{Open Source}: Freely available implementation facilitating community use and validation.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Relevance to \textit{Bioinformatics}:}

This manuscript fits well within the scope of \textit{Bioinformatics} as it presents a novel computational method with demonstrated biological application. The tool directly addresses an identified bottleneck in structural bioinformatics and enables new research directions in glycoprotein engineering, vaccine design, and therapeutic glycosylation.
